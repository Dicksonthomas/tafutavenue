Venue Booking System — For Class Representatives & Staff

How to register, find a free venue on your own campus, book it, keep track of what's been approved, and stay on top of notifications and announcements — on the web or as an installed app.

Audience Class Representatives (CR) & Staff · Covers Morogoro Main, Dar es Salaam, Tanga, Mbeya · Companion document Admin Manual (Part 2) · Live system tafutavenue-web.vercel.app

1.1Creating your account

Go to the Register page. You don't choose your own login email — the system builds it for you from your name and Reg No, so it always matches your official student identity.

1Full Name — enter your complete name exactly as registered with the university.

2Reg No — enter it in full, e.g. 14322055/T.25. The last two digits after the dot must be your intake year; the system shows a live preview of the email it will create, such as firstname.lastname25@mustudent.ac.tz.

3If your Reg No isn't accepted (too old an intake year, or the year hasn't opened for registration yet — new intakes unlock every October), use Enter Email Manually instead, or ask an Admin to register you.

4Phone, Campus, Sex, Faculty, Department, Program, Level, and Year of Study — fill these in; Program offers a live search drawn from the real teaching timetable.

5Submit. Your password is generated automatically and shown to you once, right on screen — save it, since it isn't emailed and won't be shown again.

Your account needs Admin approval first
Registering doesn't sign you in right away. Your campus Admin must approve the account before you can log in — you'll see a "pending approval" message if you try to log in before that happens. Approval is usually quick; check back or contact your Admin if it's taking a while.

If registration is closed for your campus (e.g. the intake period for CRs has ended), the Register page will show a plain message instead of the form — contact your Admin if you believe this is a mistake.

1.2Logging in

Enter your email and the password from registration, then Sign In. First thing after logging in, go to Change Password and set one you'll remember. Once signed in, you stay signed in — there's no need to log in again each time you close the app, on the web or on your phone, until you explicitly log out.

Wrong password?
The error message stays on screen until you dismiss it, refresh, or a minute passes — it won't vanish on you mid-read.
"Our system is under maintenance"
If you see this message when logging in, the Admin has temporarily paused ordinary use of the system for maintenance. Try again after the time shown, or contact your Admin.
"Your account has been suspended"
This means an Admin has temporarily suspended CR (or Staff) accounts on your campus, usually to resolve an operational issue — it is not the same as a rejected registration. Contact your Admin; access is restored once the issue is resolved.

1.3Dashboard

Your Dashboard is the first thing you see after logging in: a quick count of your bookings, what's coming up next, and a short activity feed covering your own actions plus other CRs' booking activity on your campus.

1.4Booking a venue

Open Find Venue — the main booking screen.

1Choose the Semester, Date, and the Start/End time you need.

2The system lists every venue genuinely free for that window — already checked against the official timetable and everyone else's bookings, so nothing shown can clash. Each result also shows the fuller stretch it's free for (e.g. "Free 09:00–12:00").

3Pick a venue and choose a Purpose: Study Unit, Test, Makeup Class, Meeting, or Other. Add an optional title.

4Draw your Digital Signature on the pad — required to confirm the booking.

5Confirm Booking. If nothing needs extra review, you're approved immediately and a confirmation email arrives right away.

Study Unit bookings have fixed hours
Each campus sets its own allowed time window per day of the week for Study Unit bookings. If your time falls outside it, you'll be told the allowed window for that day.

1.5The 2-a-day rule

Each CR gets two bookings per day approved automatically. A third on the same day works differently:

Beyond 2 bookings in one day
A Reason box appears in the booking form. Explain briefly why you need another venue that day. Instead of auto-approving, the request goes to your campus Admin to review.

You'll see it as Pending in My Bookings until an Admin approves or rejects it — and you'll get a notification the moment they decide (see §1.10).

1.6My Bookings & signing

Every booking you've made lives in My Bookings, each showing its status: Pending Approved Rejected Cancelled

  • If an Admin (rather than the automatic system) approved it, you'll see "Approved by [name]" under the status.
  • If it needed a reason under the 2-a-day rule, your reason is shown on the card.
  • A rejected booking always shows the Admin's rejection reason.
  • While still Pending, press Cancel Booking to withdraw it yourself.
  • If a booking is Approved but not yet signed, an Add Digital Signature button appears — this happens for bookings an Admin approved after review.

Only your 9 most recent bookings show at first, to keep the page light — press Show More below the list to reveal older ones.

1.7Venues, timetable & lecturers

All Venues

Browse every usable venue on your campus, searchable by name, code, or building — useful for getting your bearings, not for checking live availability (use Find Venue for that). Only 9 show at first; press Show More to reveal the rest.

Booked Venues

Pick any date to see everything already occupying venues that day — both official lectures and other CRs' bookings. Each list (Timetable and Bookings) starts at 9 items with its own Show More button.

Lecturer Timetable

Type a lecturer's name to pull up their entire weekly teaching schedule — venue, day, and time. A busy lecturer's schedule also starts at 9 entries, with Show More to see the full week.

1.8Profile & settings

My Profile shows your registered details. Settings lets you pick a personal accent color for your own account. Change Password is under the account menu at any time.

1.9Installing & using it offline

On a phone, use your browser's Add to Home Screen option (an Install App button also appears in the top bar when available) to add the system as its own icon, like a native app.

What still works without signal
Once you've opened the app online at least once, pages and data you've already viewed — your bookings, the venue list, settings — stay readable with no connection. A banner reading "You're offline" appears, and a "Back online" note confirms when reconnected.

Actions that reach the server — making a new booking, signing, saving anything — still need a live connection, and will tell you plainly you're offline rather than failing silently or logging you out.

1.10Notifications

The bell icon in the top bar shows how many notifications you haven't read yet, as a number. Click it to open your Notifications page.

  • Booking Approved / Booking Rejected — sent the moment your booking is decided, whether automatically or by an Admin. A rejection always includes the reason.
  • Announcement — a message your campus Admin has broadcast to CRs (e.g. a schedule change or general notice).

Filter by type, read/unread status, a specific date, or search by keyword. Click any notification to mark it read and reveal its full message — or use Mark all as read to clear everything at once.

You can also delete a notification you no longer need from this list — this only removes it from your own inbox.

For Staff

1.11Registering as Staff

Staff register separately from Class Representatives, using the Staff tab on the Register page.

1Title (optional) — choose Mr, Mrs, Ms, Dr, Prof, Eng, or CPA. If set, your greeting throughout the system reads, for example, “Hello, Dr. Kilima” instead of just your first name.

2Staff ID / Payroll No, Official Email, Phone, Position, and Campus — unlike CR registration, you choose your own email and there is no academic profile to fill in.

3Submit. Your password is generated automatically and shown to you once, on screen — save it. As with CR accounts, a Staff account also needs Admin approval before you can log in.

Staff registration can be closed
An Admin may close Staff self-registration for a campus, either immediately or for a scheduled period. If it's closed when you visit the Register page, you'll see a plain message instead of the form.

1.12Booking a venue as Staff

Staff use the same Find Venue screen as CRs, with one difference: there is no fixed Study Unit time window to work around. Two quick presets are offered — Daytime (06:00–18:00) and Evening (18:00–22:00) — or set any start/end time of your own below them. Weekends (Saturday/Sunday) are allowed. A venue is offered as long as it's genuinely free for the time you choose, checked the same way as for CRs.

Staff do not have a 2-a-day limit — every booking that finds a genuinely free venue is confirmed immediately, the same digital-signature step included.

1.13What Staff cannot see

Staff accounts never see CR/student data anywhere in the system — the sidebar, venues, bookings, and notifications a Staff member sees are all scoped to Staff activity only.

§ Booking status glossary

StatusMeaning
PendingWaiting on a campus Admin — always a 3rd-or-later booking that day, with a reason attached.
ApprovedConfirmed, either instantly by the system or by an Admin. Check for "Approved by [name]" to tell which.
RejectedDeclined by an Admin, with a reason shown to you.
CancelledWithdrawn by you while it was still Pending.

§ Troubleshooting

"I don't see the venue I'm looking for."

Find Venue only lists venues genuinely free for the exact date and time chosen. Try Booked Venues for that date, or widen your time range.

"My booking needs a reason I didn't expect."

You've already got two bookings on that date — see §1.5.

"The page looks stuck offline."

Pages and data you've opened before stay viewable with no connection, but anything that saves to the server needs to be online. Reconnect and try again.

"I got a notification but I'm not sure what it means."

Click it — it expands to show the full message. If it's an Announcement, it was sent by your campus Admin to every CR, not to you specifically.
